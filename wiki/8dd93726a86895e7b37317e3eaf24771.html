I went swimming in Gold River with two friends and got swept out into a fast current. Where the two rivers came together, I went under the first time. I tried waving at my friends because I could not swim to shore. So, I tried going with the current but got sucked under again. I came up for third time but saw bright lights and bubbles rising above. Then everything was quiet.

The next thing was that I was about a few hundred feet in air watching my body tumble down the river. I thought how weird it was that I had drowned; yet I could still see, think, and wonder. I thought, 'OMG, here we go again. We don't die. All the hogwash about heaven and hell isn't real. I just drop my body off and, wow! I am not dead.' As I rose higher and higher, I lost vision of my body. Then I turned around and saw the light.

I was taken to a big room and shown myself on a screen for a life reading. I saw everything in my life that I had done, frame by frame, and in slow motion. I re-experienced every moment and its cause and effect. There was a sense of oneness, no judgment, no criticism, and no condemnation. There was just a sense of peace. Then it was over and I was told that I had to go back. As I turned and descended, I saw my body caught on a tree that was part in and out of water.

As I slowly approached, I saw that my head was out of the water. As I came into my body, I was pulling myself out of water by pulling on the branches. I don't recall much about getting up and walking back to town, except that I was very cold. the first house that I recognized was a friend that I knew, so I knocked on door. Her little sister was the only one home and asked if I could get warm. Apparently, I collapsed on the couch and slept for 3 days, only getting up to go to the bathroom and drink tea. At least 6 hours had passed since my friends had last seen me. My friends didn't know that I had drowned; they thought I was waving to them to join me.
